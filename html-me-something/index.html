Tight-fisted Alice Noris

A Short Story by Ken (and Random Plot Generator)

Alice Noris had always loved comfortable Castle Greyskull with its horrible, harsh hills. It was a place where she felt unstable.

She was a tight-fisted, peculiar, cocoa drinker with fluffy abs and beautiful fingers. Her friends saw her as a flat, flaky friend. Once, she had even helped a rotten baby bird recover from a flying accident. That's the sort of woman he was.

Alice walked over to the window and reflected on her cozy surroundings. The drizzle rained like partying toads.

Then she saw something in the distance, or rather someone. It was the figure of Tommy Kowalski. Tommy was a modest lover with greasy abs and wobbly fingers.

Alice gulped. She was not prepared for Tommy.

As Alice stepped outside and Tommy came closer, she could see the crooked glint in his eye.

Tommy gazed with the affection of 7869 selfish obnoxious ostriches. He said, in hushed tones, "I love you and I want Internet access."

Alice looked back, even more happy and still fingering the minuscule rock. "Tommy, póg mo thóin," she replied.

They looked at each other with sleepy feelings, like two defeated, decaying dogs sitting at a very forgetful snow storm, which had drum and bass music playing in the background and two caring uncles smiling to the beat.

Alice regarded Tommy's greasy abs and wobbly fingers. "I feel the same way!" revealed Alice with a delighted grin.

Tommy looked stressed, his emotions blushing like a bored, big blade.

Then Tommy came inside for a nice mug of cocoa.
